01 — About

Dynamic Mathematician
Shaping the Future

A passionate mathematician and researcher committed to bridging abstract mathematical theory with real-world biochemical and computational challenges. Completed my Ph.D. at VIT Vellore with a thesis on "Mathematical Treatment to the Models that Emerges in Biochemical Systems."

My work explores the intersection of nonlinear mathematics, enzyme kinetics, and machine learning — developing semi-analytical methods and Physics-Informed Neural Networks to solve complex reaction-diffusion problems that matter in biomedical science.

As an educator, I believe mathematics is not a subject to be delivered — it is an experience to be discovered. I build every lesson from first principles, training students to think independently, question deeply, and develop genuine mathematical intuition rather than surface-level familiarity with formulas.

I teach with geometry, story, and connection — making abstract ideas visible and real. Whether it is the elegance of Linear Algebra or the motion hidden inside a Differential Equation, I always lead with the why before the how, so that understanding becomes lasting and not just momentary.

Beyond lectures, I invest in my students through problem-solving sessions, research mentoring, and computational training — building thinkers who are analytically rigorous, computationally confident, and genuinely passionate about the beauty of mathematics.
